Specialized Focus

Youth & Adolescent Psychosocial Support

Our psychosocial programs are designed specifically to address the unique mental health and emotional wellbeing needs of young people

Anxiety & Depression

Rising rates of mental health challenges among adolescents require targeted, age-appropriate interventions.

Trauma & Adversity

Many youth have experienced violence, loss, or displacement requiring trauma-informed support.

Social Isolation

Post-pandemic isolation and lack of safe peer connections impact adolescent development.

Academic & Career Pressure

Unmanaged stress from performance expectations affects mental wellbeing.

Our Youth Psychosocial Programs

Resilience Building for Adolescents

Empowering young people with coping strategies, emotional regulation skills, and the ability to bounce back from adversity.

2-5 days Ages 13-19

Peer Support Leadership Training

Training youth to become effective peer supporters who can provide emotional first aid and referral guidance to their peers.

3-5 days Youth ages 16-25

Trauma-Informed Care for Youth Workers

Equipping teachers, social workers, and youth mentors with skills to support traumatized adolescents in their care.

3 days Youth serving professionals

Adolescent Mental Health Awareness

Raising awareness about common mental health challenges among youth and reducing stigma in communities.

1-2 days Youth, parents, community leaders

Life Skills & Emotional Wellbeing

Integrating psychosocial support with practical life skills for holistic adolescent development.

5-10 days In-school & out-of-school youth

Crisis Support & Referral Systems

Establishing youth-friendly referral pathways and crisis support mechanisms in schools and communities.

3-5 days Institutions & organizations
