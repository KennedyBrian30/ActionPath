Why It Matters

A strategy that sits in a drawer is not a strategy

Too many development organizations operate on outdated strategies, unclear theories of change, or no documented direction at all — relying on institutional memory and donor project cycles as their de facto plan. The result is reactive programming, missed opportunities, and staff without a shared sense of purpose.

ActionPath Africa's Strategic Planning & Management service helps organizations step back, assess their position honestly, and build forward-looking strategies that are grounded in evidence, owned by their teams, and designed to actually be implemented.

We don't deliver strategies as external experts and leave. We facilitate the process so your people own the outcome — and we stay engaged through year-one implementation to ensure the strategy gets off the ground.

Our Philosophy

Four Principles Behind Every Engagement

Participatory

We facilitate, not dictate. Every strategy is built through inclusive processes that bring staff, leadership, and community voices into the room.

Evidence-Based

Strategy built on data — organizational assessments, stakeholder consultations, environmental scans, and program evidence rather than assumptions.

Context-Grounded

We don't import frameworks from outside. Every strategy is designed for your organization's specific operating environment, history, and capacity.

Adaptive

Strategies include built-in review mechanisms — ensuring organizations can adjust course as contexts change rather than being locked into outdated plans.

What We Offer

Our Strategic Planning Service Areas

Six integrated service areas covering organizational strategy from vision to day-to-day management.

Organizational Strategy Development

Facilitating inclusive strategic planning processes that produce clear, measurable, and time-bound strategies co-owned by staff, leadership, and stakeholders.

Theory of Change Development

Building rigorous Theories of Change that trace the logic from activities to outcomes — creating a shared organizational narrative of how change happens.

Operational Planning & Work Planning

Translating strategy into actionable annual work plans with clear ownership, timelines, and resource requirements — bridging vision and day-to-day execution.

Program Design & Proposal Development

Designing fundable, evidence-based programs and writing compelling donor proposals that accurately represent your organization's capacity and community needs.

Organizational Governance & Structures

Reviewing and strengthening board structures, policy frameworks, and decision-making systems to ensure organizations are well-governed and legally compliant.

Change Management & Organizational Learning

Supporting organizations navigating transitions — restructuring, program pivots, scaling, or leadership change — with structured change management processes.

Our Process

Six Steps to a Strategy That Works

A structured, participatory engagement process that produces a strategy document and the organizational buy-in to implement it.

01

Organizational Assessment

We begin with a structured assessment of your organization — reviewing strategy, governance, finances, programs, culture, and stakeholder relationships to understand where you are before planning where you're going.

02

Environmental & Context Scan

A SWOT analysis, stakeholder mapping, and sector landscape review that grounds your strategy in the real operating environment — not wishful thinking.

03

Visioning & Priority Setting

Facilitated workshops with leadership, staff, and key stakeholders that generate shared vision, strategic priorities, and a Theory of Change that everyone owns.

04

Strategy Drafting

We produce a clear, compelling strategy document — with vision, mission, values, strategic pillars, objectives, and indicators — ready for board review and approval.

05

Operational Planning

Translating the strategy into year-one work plans, budgets, and accountability frameworks so implementation begins immediately with clear ownership.

06

Launch & Capacity Building

Supporting the strategy launch, training leadership and staff on strategic management, and establishing quarterly review processes that keep the strategy alive.
