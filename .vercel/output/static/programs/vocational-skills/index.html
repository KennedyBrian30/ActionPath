The Challenge

A certified skill is one of the most reliable pathways out of poverty

Across Africa's most vulnerable communities, unemployment is not a talent problem. It is a certification problem. The trades exist. The demand exists. What is missing is structured, market aligned training with credentials that employers and buyers recognize and trust.

For youth exiting conflict zones, for people living with HIV/AIDS seeking dignified work, and for households trapped in extreme poverty, a nationally recognized vocational certificate is transformative. It signals competence. It unlocks employment. It provides the foundation for self employment.

We do not just teach a trade. We certify it. We connect it to real markets. We equip graduates with the business and employability skills to sustain income from it for years. This is not charity. This is capability building.

What We Teach

Six Market Driven Trade Tracks

Every trade track is selected based on local market demand analysis. We train for jobs and contracts that actually exist in your community. No guesswork. No dead ends.

Tailoring and Garment Production

Master industrial sewing, pattern making, and garment finishing. Graduates secure school uniform contracts, open tailoring shops, or sell through online fashion markets.

Carpentry and Furniture Making

Learn hand tool and machine carpentry, furniture design, and eco friendly bamboo production. Demand is high for local and export markets.

Solar Installation and Electrical

Hands on training in solar panel installation, wiring, and maintenance. This is one of Africa's fastest growing technical sectors with chronic skills shortages.

Eco Friendly Production

Produce reusable bags, recycled material crafts, and sustainable packaging. Tap into the rapidly growing demand for green products across Africa.

Machine Operation and Repair

Train on industrial sewing machines, agricultural equipment, and basic automotive mechanics. This track leads directly to formal employment.

Construction and Masonry

Master brick laying, plastering, tiling, and construction project basics. The building sector is booming and certified masons are in high demand.

Shared Production Workspaces

Training on real tools, in real production environments

One of the biggest barriers to vocational skills uptake is that graduates leave training with knowledge but no access to equipment. ActionPath Africa solves this by establishing shared production workspaces equipped with industrial tools, machines, and materials. You learn on the same machines you will use to earn.

Industrial sewing machines for tailoring graduates ready for production volume
Woodworking benches and power tools for carpenters to build real furniture
Solar installation demo kits and practice arrays for hands on electrical training
18 months post graduation shared access. Graduates pay only a nominal usage fee.

Our Approach

From Raw Potential to Certified Professional

A six stage pathway that transforms community members into credentialed, market connected tradespeople. Each step builds on the last until you are ready to earn.

01

Trade and Market Assessment

We identify which trades have genuine local demand before training begins. No hypothetical jobs. No wasted effort. We train for markets that actually exist.

02

Accredited Partner Enrollment

Participants enroll at accredited TVET institutions where ActionPath subsidizes fees and provides psychosocial support alongside technical training.

03

Practical Workshops

Hands on production in ActionPath shared workspaces equipped with industrial tools. This bridges the gap between classroom knowledge and real work.

04

Business and Employability Skills

Alongside the trade, participants learn CV writing, basic bookkeeping, customer service, and workplace communication. We produce complete professionals.

05

Market Showcase and Job Linking

A graduation showcase where local employers, buyers, and community leaders see participants' work and make direct hiring or purchase commitments.

06

Post Graduation Support

Shared workspace access, peer networks, and quarterly check ins for 18 months after graduation. We stay with you until you are earning.

Partnership Ecosystem

Built on a Four Partner Model

Successful vocational training requires more than a classroom. Our four partner model connects training to certification, employment, markets, and infrastructure. No single organization can do this alone.

TVET Institutions

Nationally accredited technical vocational colleges delivering certified curriculum that employers trust.

Local Employers

Manufacturing, construction, and service sector companies that are actively hiring trained graduates.

Buyer Networks

Tailoring cooperatives, furniture buyers, and eco product distributors purchasing graduate output at fair prices.

Tool Suppliers

Equipment partners providing machines, tools, and shared production workspace infrastructure.
